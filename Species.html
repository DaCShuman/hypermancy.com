The S3 Storytelling System

The MDS stresses the use of a basic S3 system to assist in writing stories. All the Paths of Power modules will utilize the S3 system and the MDS core book will include a guide for new Storytellers.
What does S3 stand for?
Story, Scene, Situation. Importantly, this sets a standard for Adventure Point payout. Adventure Points are to be utilized to advance character traits. A story is rated by the complexity of its scenes and upon completion delivers a mass payout of Adventure Points and likewise, each Scene is rated for a Adventure Point pay out based on the challenges and contests planned within. A big factor in the S3 system is to give the players agency to determine how they want to navigate a Scene.
Whether the player group opts to infiltrate and avoid combat through stealth, rely on guile and wit to talk their way through, or... just... kill everything, the group will get a payout based on completion with bonuses for discoveries
The MDS does its best to give players the capability to play the way they wish to and avoids a complication tied to the loss of Adventure Points, should the enemies be circumvented, rather than destroyed whole sale.

This all may seem a tad bit elementary to seasoned writers, but our goal is to spread knowledge and teach new storytellers how to bridge the gap between their imagination to the tabletop, without abusing anyone with an insane learning curve. Should anyone want to create their own standalone story, we at Hypermancy will provide the foundation to do your own thing. Meanwhile, Paths of Power will feature modules ready to play, built from the S3 system with the intention of teaching the Magnitude Dice System.
